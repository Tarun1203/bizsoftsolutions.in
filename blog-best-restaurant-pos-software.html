Best Restaurant POS Software (2026): What Actually Matters at Service Time

By BizSoft Solutions · Updated June 2026 · 7 min read

A restaurant POS is judged in one moment: Saturday night, full house, three Zomato orders beeping, and a table asking to split the bill. Software that survives that moment is good. Everything else is brochure talk. Here's what to evaluate, in order of importance.

1. KOT speed and routing (the #1 factor)

The kitchen order ticket is the heartbeat of a restaurant. Test: punch a 6-item order with one modifier ("less spicy") — does it hit the right station printers (tandoor vs chinese vs bar) in under 5 seconds? Can a captain transfer items between tables? Can a manager-PIN void an item with a reason log? If any answer is no, walk away regardless of price.

2. Online order integration

If Zomato/Swiggy orders don't land inside the POS, your staff re-punches every order manually — errors, delays, and sales reports that never match. Native integration means auto-accept, item availability sync (86'd items go off the app instantly), and one consolidated day-end report. This feature alone decides POS choice for delivery-heavy outlets.

3. Recipe-level inventory (where profit hides)

Restaurants don't lose money on sales — they lose it in the kitchen. A POS that deducts ingredients per dish sold gives you a daily theoretical-vs-actual consumption variance. A 4% paneer variance, caught weekly, often pays the entire year's software cost. Item-level stock (like retail apps do) cannot do this.

4. Fraud & leakage control

  • Discount and void alerts to the owner's phone, with reason codes
  • Reprint logs (the classic "reprint and pocket cash" trick)
  • Cashier-wise settlement matching cash/card/UPI to bills
  • Role-based permissions — waiters can't edit prices

5. Offline reliability

Billing and KOT must run on the local network even when broadband dies. Cloud-only POS systems freeze at the worst moments. Hybrid (local billing + cloud reports) is the right architecture for India.

What about GST?

Any serious POS handles 5% restaurant GST, separate MRP-item rates, and service charge configuration. It's necessary but not differentiating — don't let a salesperson sell "GST billing" as a restaurant feature.
