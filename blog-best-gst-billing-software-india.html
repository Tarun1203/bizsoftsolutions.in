Best GST Billing Software in India (2026): An Honest Ranking

By BizSoft Solutions · Updated June 2026 · 8 min read

We sell billing software for a living, and we'll tell you something most listicles won't: there is no single "best" billing software. There is only the best one for your size, trade and habits. Below is the ranking we actually use when advising the 1,500+ businesses we've served.

What "GST billing software" must do (the non-negotiables)

  • Generate GST-compliant tax invoices with correct HSN/SAC, CGST/SGST/IGST split
  • Produce GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B-ready data your CA can file from
  • Track stock so billing and inventory never disagree
  • Work reliably offline — internet in Indian shops is not a given
  • Back up your data automatically

1. Vyapar — best overall for small retail & trading

Vyapar wins for the majority of small shops because it nails the basics with the least learning curve: 30-second invoices, item-wise stock with low-stock alerts, UPI QR on bills, WhatsApp payment reminders, and clean GSTR exports. It's offline-first and the mobile + desktop combo fits how owners actually work.

Pricing: roughly ₹3,400–4,000/year depending on plan and offers (see live partner pricing). Weakness: not a full accounting suite — no audit-grade statutory modules.

2. TallyPrime — best for accountant-driven businesses

If your business revolves around an accountant, audits, and complex books (multiple branches, manufacturing with job work, TDS/TCS), Tally remains the gold standard. But it's overkill — and over-budget at ₹18,000+ one-time or ₹750+/month rental — for a shop that mainly needs fast billing. Read our detailed Tally vs Vyapar breakdown.

3. myBillBook — solid Vyapar alternative

Very similar feature set to Vyapar with a polished app. We find Vyapar's offline desktop experience and report depth slightly ahead, and partner support matters more than the brand. If you're choosing between them, the deciding factors are usually price at renewal and the support you get locally.

4. Zoho Books — best for service businesses & startups

Zoho Books is excellent cloud accounting — GST filing integration, bank feeds, automation. But it assumes you live in a browser with good internet, and inventory/billing speed at a physical counter is not its game. Great for agencies, consultants and online-first companies; rarely our pick for a shop counter.

5. Specialized POS — when generic billing is the wrong question

If you run a restaurant, you need KOT, tables and Zomato/Swiggy integration — that's a restaurant POS, not billing software. If you run a liquor shop, you need size-wise stock and excise registers — that's liquor management software. Buying generic billing software for these trades is the most common ₹4,000 mistake we see.
