Billing Software for Small Business: The Buyer's Guide Nobody Gives You

By BizSoft Solutions · Updated June 2026 · 7 min read

Every week we meet shop owners who bought billing software and stopped using it within a month. The software wasn't bad — the buying decision was. This guide is the checklist we wish every owner read before spending money.

Step 1: Write down your actual workflow (10 minutes)

Before comparing apps, note: How many bills a day? Barcode or manual? Who bills — you or staff? Do you give credit (udhaar)? Do you need stock tracking, or just bills? Mobile, desktop, or both? GST registered or composition? Every "best software" claim is meaningless until matched against this list.

Step 2: The must-have features (don't pay for less)

  • Fast billing — under 30 seconds per bill, with barcode support if you have packaged goods
  • GST invoice formats — plus the ability to bill non-GST customers cleanly
  • Stock that updates with every bill — and low-stock alerts
  • Party ledgers — outstanding tracking with payment reminders (this alone recovers the software cost)
  • Offline working — billing must never depend on internet
  • Automatic backup — device theft/crash should not mean data death
  • Reports your CA accepts — GSTR-1/3B exports, sale/purchase registers

Step 3: The 7 mistakes that kill software purchases

  1. Buying the famous one, not the fitting one. Tally for a 50-bill-a-day kirana is like buying a truck for grocery runs. (See Tally vs Vyapar.)
  2. Ignoring the trade-specific need. Restaurants need KOT (restaurant POS); liquor shops need excise registers (liquor software). Generic apps fail these trades on day one.
  3. Falling for "free" software. Free tiers cap invoices/features exactly when you're hooked. Calculate the paid-plan price before starting.
  4. Not checking renewal price. Year-1 discounts can double at renewal. Ask for the renewal rate in writing.
  5. Skipping data import. If your 2,000 items and party balances aren't imported, staff will abandon the software in a week. Make import a condition of purchase.
  6. No training for staff. The owner learns the app; the staff bills all day. Untrained staff = "software is slow" complaints = back to the bill book.
  7. Buying online with zero support. The license is identical; the difference is who picks up the phone when the printer misbehaves on a Saturday evening. Buy where support lives.

Step 4: Budget realistically

Good small-business billing software costs ₹3,000–5,000/year. A restaurant POS runs ₹10,000–18,000/year. Liquor management ₹12,000–20,000/year. Hardware (printer, scanner, drawer) is one-time ₹8,000–25,000. If a quote is far below these ranges, you're usually looking at a feature-capped tier.

The software that gets used daily at ₹4,000/year is infinitely cheaper than the one gathering dust at ₹2,000/year.

Step 5: Demand a demo on YOUR data

A canned demo proves nothing. Insist the seller demos with your items, your menu, your brands. Ten minutes of billing your actual products reveals more than an hour of feature tours. (Yes, we do this free — it's how we'd want to buy, too.)
