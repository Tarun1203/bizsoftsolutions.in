2. Feature-by-Feature Comparison

📱 Billing & Invoicing

Vyapar leads here for Indian SMBs — it offers GST invoices, proforma, delivery challan, purchase orders, and estimates right from your phone. Invoices can be shared via WhatsApp instantly. My BillBook is also easy for invoicing but lacks depth. Tally Prime has the most powerful invoicing but requires a dedicated accountant to operate. Zoho Books offers professional templates with multi-currency support — great for exporters.

📦 Inventory Management

Vyapar and Tally Prime both offer strong inventory with stock tracking, low-stock alerts, and item-wise reports. My BillBook has basic inventory suitable only for small product catalogs. Zoho Books handles inventory well but pairs better with Zoho Inventory for warehouse needs.

📊 Reports & Analytics

Tally Prime remains the king of reports — Balance Sheet, P&L, Day Book, Trial Balance — all in real-time. Zoho Books matches this with dashboards. Vyapar covers all essential SMB reports (profit, stock, party ledger, cash flow). My BillBook provides only basic summary reports.

4. GST & Compliance

All four handle basic GST invoicing, but the depth varies significantly:

  • Vyapar — GSTR-1, GSTR-3B JSON export, e-Invoice (IRN), e-Way Bill, HSN/SAC codes, RCM, composite scheme. Handles 90% of SMB GST needs.
  • Tally Prime — The most comprehensive GST engine in India. Handles complex scenarios: reverse charge, import/export, ISD, annual reconciliation. Best for CA-managed businesses.
  • My BillBook — Basic GST invoicing only. No GSTR exports, no e-invoice. Not suitable for businesses that file returns independently.
  • Zoho Books — Full GST compliance including GSTR-1/2A/3B reconciliation, e-Invoice, e-Way Bill, TDS. Excellent for service businesses and exporters.
